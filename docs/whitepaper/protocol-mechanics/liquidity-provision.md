Liquidity Providers (LPs) in Likwid Protocol gain exposure to two distinct yet synergistic income streams by contributing assets to structured pools. This design optimizes capital efficiency while diversifying risk profiles across trading activity and credit markets. Below we explore the mechanics and interdependencies of these revenue sources in detail.

### **​1. Swap Fee Revenue: Market-Driven Earnings​**

Swap fees constitute the primary income stream derived directly from trading activity within Likwid’s liquidity pools. This model aligns with traditional AMMs but introduces nuanced optimizations specific to Likwid’s architecture:

* **Dynamic Fee Tiers**:\
  Protocol-wide base fees start at 0.30% but fluctuate based on real-time market volatility and liquidity depth. During periods of high volatility (e.g., >5% hourly price swings), fees scale upward by 15-40% to compensate LPs for increased arbitrage risk. Conversely, stable market conditions trigger fee reductions to attract volume. This dynamic equilibriums ensures optimal fee capture while maintaining competitive slippage.
* ​**Proportional Distribution Mechanics**:\
  Fees accumulate in pool-denominated tokens and distribute pro-rata to LPs based on their percentage ownership of the liquidity pool. An LP contributing 5% of a pool’s total USDC/ETH liquidity receives exactly 5% of all fees generated by swaps in that pool. Distributions compound hourly via reinvestment mechanisms, creating exponential yield growth during high-volume epochs.

### **2: Interest from Borrowed or Margin ​**

When users deposit assets into Likwid's liquidity pools, their funds serve dual purposes simultaneously:

* Providing swap liquidity for traders exchanging tokens (e.g., ETH ⇄ USDC)
* Funding borrowing activities for lenders and margin traders
